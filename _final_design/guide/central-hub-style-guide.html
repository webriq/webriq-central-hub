1 · Brand foundations

The Hub is an internal tool people live in all day. It borrows WebriQ's official website colors but expresses them as a working surface: navy is chrome and ink, blue means "interactive," orange means "act now." Color always carries meaning — never decoration.

3 · Typography

Three faces, three jobs. Space Grotesk gives the Hub its voice but appears only at display sizes. Inter does all UI work. JetBrains Mono is reserved for machine values — day counters, dates, IDs, keyboard shortcuts — so scannable data always looks like data.

5 · Components

The working vocabulary. Every interactive element ships with default, hover, focus-visible, active, and disabled states — transitions at 160ms ease-out.

6 · Motion & interaction

Motion conveys state, never decorates. People load the Hub into a task — no page-load choreography, no staggered entrances.

  • · Transitions: 160ms cubic-bezier(.22,1,.36,1) on background, color, border-color. 120ms on press (1px translateY).
  • · Animate only compositor-friendly properties — never layout (width, height, top).
  • · Hover on rows: background tint to --blue-50. No lifts, no scale.
  • · Focus: 2px blue outline, 2px offset — visible on every interactive element, always.
  • · Loading: skeleton rows in place, never a centered spinner.
  • · prefers-reduced-motion: all transitions collapse to instant. Non-negotiable.

7 · Voice & tone

The Hub speaks like a reliable coworker: direct, specific, calm under deadline pressure.

  • · Sentence case everywhere except table headers and mono data labels (caps).
  • · Buttons name the outcome: "Start handover," "Manage permissions," "Export weekly report."
  • · Reminders state the who + what + when: "Day 16 handover — Coastal Dental · Due Thu, Jul 23."
  • · Programme vocabulary is fixed: Onboard, Migrate & Rebrand, Publish, AI Visibility, Optimize. Days are "Day 34," never "d34" or "34th day."
  • · Empty states invite action: "No clients in Publish yet — they'll appear here from Day 31."
  • · No exclamation points in primary UI. No apologies in errors — state what happened and the fix.

8 · Do's & don'ts

✓ Do

  • One orange CTA per screen, maximum
  • Border + soft shadow on every raised surface
  • Mono face for any day count, date, ID, or shortcut
  • Phase widths always to true day scale (15/15/30/30/30)
  • Navy for filter/selection states, blue for actions
  • Full-radius pills for buttons and tracks
  • Skeletons over spinners; empty states that teach

✕ Don't

  • No colored left-border accent stripes on cards or list items
  • No gradient text, no glassmorphism, no decorative blur
  • No Space Grotesk in buttons, labels, or table cells
  • No phase hue reused for a non-phase meaning
  • No orange for anything that isn't a call to action
  • No nested cards; no shadow-only elevation
  • No page-load animation sequences
